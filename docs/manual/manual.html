1

Getting started

The portal is a single website that the whole school shares. What you see depends on who you are. You never have to set anything up — you just sign in, and the right screen opens for you.

If you are a…You can…
StudentSee your own marks, attendance and progress, read announcements, check the calendar, and give a reason for any absence.
Parent / GuardianSee each of your children in one place, open their detailed progress, and explain their absences.
TeacherMark attendance for your class, enter and release marks, and view the progress of your class and subjects.
PrincipalSee the whole school — averages, attendance, and which students need support — without editing marks.
Office / AdminManage the school — students, teacher assignments, marks, reports, and moving students to the next class.
The golden rule
You can only ever see and change what belongs to you. A subject teacher sees their subjects; a class teacher sees their whole class; a parent sees only their own children. This keeps everyone's data private and safe.
2

How to log in

  • Open the portal in any web browser on a phone, tablet or computer.
  • On the right you'll see three ways to sign in — Google, Microsoft and Zoho. Tap the one your school gave you an account with.
  • Under “Demo logins” there are ready-made accounts (Principal, Teacher, Parent, Student, Office). Tapping one signs you straight in — handy for trying things out or for a demonstration.
  • Once you sign in, your own dashboard opens automatically. You will not see anyone else's private information.
On a phone
The picture on the left is hidden on small screens so the sign-in options fill the whole screen. Everything still works exactly the same.
3

Finding your way around

Every screen shares the same simple layout, so once you learn it once, you know it everywhere.

  • The menu (left side): your links — Dashboard, Attendance, Marks, Progress, Announcements, Calendar, Reports and more. On a phone, tap the ☰ button at the top to open the menu.
  • Your name card (top-right of the dashboard): shows your name and role. Tap it to sign out — a confirmation box appears so you never sign out by accident.
  • Language: parents can switch between English, Hindi and Bengali using the language box next to the name card.
  • Coloured icons mark each section so it's easy to spot the one you want.
Tip
Anything that is a link will gently highlight when you move your pointer over it (or tap it). If it highlights, you can open it.
4

The Student view

  • Your name and class card sits at the top-right, with your roll number, class-section and class teacher.
  • Quick links take you straight to your Progress, Attendance, Announcements and Calendar.
  • Announcements and Events keep you up to date with school notices and important dates.

Seeing your progress

  • See your average and how each subject is going, exam by exam.
  • Use the session picker at the top to look at this year, an earlier year, or your whole history in one view.
  • Only released results appear — a teacher chooses when marks become visible.
5

The Parent / Guardian view

  • Each child appears as a neat ID card showing their Class, Section and Roll number.
  • Tap the blue arrow on a card to open that child's full progress record.
  • If your child was marked absent, you'll be asked to add a reason — just a couple of taps.
  • Use the language box at the top to read everything in English, Hindi or Bengali.
6

The Teacher view

  • My Class (for class teachers) gives one-tap access to Progress, Attendance, Marks and Students for your own class.
  • Other Subject Classes lists the sections where you teach a subject — tap any to see its progress.
  • A class teacher can act as the subject teacher for any subject in their own class.

Marking attendance

  • Mark each student Present (P) or Absent (A). Use Mark all present / absent to save time.
  • Turn on Holiday for a day off — it's kept out of the attendance calculation.
  • Tap Save attendance when done. Students below the required attendance are flagged for you.

Entering marks

  • Pick the section, subject and exam, then type each student's marks (or mark them absent).
  • Marks stay a Draft (hidden from parents) until you press the black Release results button.
  • Need a new exam column? Use New exam — it appears for every section of the class.
7

The Principal view

  • The top cards show total students, the overall average, attendance %, and how many students are falling behind.
  • Every card is clickable — tap one to jump to its detail (for example, tap “Falling behind” to see the students who need support).
  • A class-by-class chart shows how each class is performing.
  • The principal can view everything but does not enter marks — that's done by teachers and the office.
8

The Office / Admin view

  • See headline numbers — students, staff, teacher assignments, and how many results are released vs. still draft.
  • A pending-work banner reminds the office of results still waiting to be released.
  • Quick links reach Students, Marks, Reports, Assigned Teachers, Promotion and more.
Moving to the next class
The Promotion screen is where the office creates the next school year and moves each class up. Class XII students graduate; a fresh Class I intake begins.
9

Everyday tasks

Progress & analytics

  • Use the filters at the top to narrow down: Year → Class → Section → Subject → Exam → Student. Each choice updates the ones after it.
  • See best performers, students needing support, grade spread and subject comparisons. Tap any student's name to open their full record.

Attendance overview (principal & office)

  • See attendance for a day or a whole month, rolled up by class and section.
  • Tap a section to open its own page with each student's attendance in a clear table.

Reports

  • Choose the report type (Monthly / Yearly / By exam) and the detail (whole class or one student).
  • A class report downloads as an Excel file; a single student report prints as a PDF.
  • The class table can be ordered by roll number or highest marks first. “Average marks percent” means the student's average across all marks in that period.

Announcements & Calendar

  • Tap any announcement to read it in full. Notices are clearly marked as coming from the Principal's Desk, School Office or a Class Teacher.
  • The calendar shows exams, activities and holidays so nothing is missed.

Leave & absence reasons

  • Students and parents can apply for leave and give a reason for any day marked absent.
  • Class teachers can review and approve these requests.
10

Good to know

  • Why can't I see my marks yet? Results appear only after the teacher releases them. Until then they're a private draft.
  • Why do I see different screens than my friend? The portal shows each role only what it needs — a student, parent, teacher and principal each see a different home screen.
  • Does it work on my phone? Yes. The whole portal adjusts to phones and tablets; the menu tucks behind the ☰ button.
  • How do I change the language? Parents can switch between English, Hindi and Bengali from the language box near their name.
  • How do I sign out? Tap your name card on the dashboard and confirm.
