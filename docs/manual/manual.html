Getting started

One portal, the right view for everyone

The portal is a single website the whole school shares. What you see depends on who you are — you never set anything up. You simply sign in, and the right screen opens for you.

StudentSee your own marks, attendance and progress, read notices, check the calendar, and give a reason for any absence.
Parent / GuardianSee each of your children in one place, open their detailed progress, and explain their absences.
TeacherMark attendance for your class, enter and release marks, and view the progress of your class and subjects.
PrincipalSee the whole school — averages, attendance, and which students need support — without editing marks.
Office / AdminManage students, teacher assignments, marks, reports, and moving students to the next class.
The guiding principle You can only ever see and change what belongs to you. A subject teacher sees their subjects; a class teacher sees their whole class; a parent sees only their own children. Everyone's data stays private.

Access

How to sign in

  • Open the portal in any browser — on a phone, tablet or computer.
  • On the right, choose how to sign in: Google, Microsoft or Zoho — whichever account your school gave you.
  • Under Demo logins there are ready-made accounts (Principal, Teacher, Parent, Student, Office). Tapping one signs you in instantly — ideal for a trial or a demonstration.
  • After signing in, your own dashboard opens automatically.
On a phone The picture on the left is hidden on small screens so the sign-in options fill the display. Everything else works exactly the same.
3
Orientation

Finding your way around

Every screen shares the same simple layout, so once you learn it, you know it everywhere.

  • The menu (left): your links — Dashboard, Attendance, Marks, Progress, Announcements, Calendar, Reports and more. On a phone, tap ☰ at the top to open it.
  • Your name card (top-right of the dashboard): shows your name and role. Tap it to sign out — a confirmation appears, so you never sign out by accident.
  • Language: parents can switch between English, Hindi and Bengali from the box beside the name card.
  • Colour-coded icons mark each section so the one you want is easy to spot.
A quick tell Anything you can open will gently highlight when you point at or tap it. If it highlights, it's a link.
4
For students

The Student view

  • Your name and class card sits at the top-right, with your roll number, class-section and class teacher.
  • Quick links jump straight to Progress, Attendance, Announcements and the Calendar.
  • Announcements and Events keep you up to date with notices and important dates.

Seeing your progress

  • See your average and how each subject is going, exam by exam.
  • Use the session picker to view this year, an earlier year, or your whole history together.
  • Only released results appear — a teacher chooses when marks become visible.
5
For parents

The Parent / Guardian view

  • Each child appears as a neat ID card showing their Class, Section and Roll number.
  • Tap the blue arrow on a card to open that child's full progress record.
  • If a child was marked absent, you'll be asked to add a reason — just a couple of taps.
  • Read everything in English, Hindi or Bengali using the language box at the top.
6
For teachers

The Teacher view

  • My Class (for class teachers) gives one-tap access to Progress, Attendance, Marks and Students for your own class.
  • Other Subject Classes lists the sections where you teach a subject — tap any to see its progress.
  • A class teacher can act as the subject teacher for any subject in their own class.

Marking attendance

  • Mark each student Present (P) or Absent (A); use Mark all present / absent to save time.
  • Turn on Holiday for a day off — it's kept out of the attendance calculation.
  • Tap Save attendance when done; students below the required attendance are flagged for you.

Entering marks

  • Pick the section, subject and exam, then type each student's marks (or mark them absent).
  • Marks stay a Draft — hidden from parents — until you press the black Release results button.
  • Need a new exam column? Use New exam; it appears for every section of the class.
7
For the principal

The Principal view

  • The top cards show total students, the overall average, attendance %, and how many students are falling behind.
  • Every card is clickable — tap one to open its detail (for example, tap “Falling behind” to see the students who need support).
  • A class-by-class chart shows how each class is performing.
  • The principal can view everything, but does not enter marks — that's done by teachers and the office.
8
For the office

The Office / Admin view

  • See headline numbers — students, staff, teacher assignments, and results released versus still in draft.
  • A pending-work banner flags results still waiting to be released.
  • Quick links reach Students, Marks, Reports, Assigned Teachers, Promotion and more.
Moving to the next class The Promotion screen is where the office creates the next school year and moves each class up. Class XII students graduate; a fresh Class I intake begins.

Reference

Everyday tasks

Progress & analytics

  • Narrow down with the filters: Year → Class → Section → Subject → Exam → Student. Each choice refines the next.
  • See best performers, students needing support, grade spread and subject comparisons. Tap any student's name to open their full record.

Attendance overview — principal & office

  • See attendance for a day or a whole month, rolled up by class and section.
  • Tap a section to open its own page with each student's attendance in a clear table.

Reports

  • Choose the type (Monthly / Yearly / By exam) and the detail (whole class or one student).
  • A class report downloads as an Excel file; a single student report prints as a PDF.
  • Order the class table by roll number or highest marks first. “Average marks percent” is the student's average across all marks in that period.

Announcements & Calendar

  • Tap an announcement to read it in full. Notices are marked as from the Principal's Desk, School Office or a Class Teacher.
  • The calendar lists exams, activities and holidays so nothing is missed.

Leave & absence reasons

  • Students and parents can apply for leave and give a reason for any day marked absent.
  • Class teachers review and approve these requests.

Help

Good to know

  • Why can't I see my marks yet?  Results appear only after the teacher releases them; until then they're a private draft.
  • Why do I see a different screen than my friend?  The portal shows each role only what it needs — student, parent, teacher and principal each get a different home screen.
  • Does it work on my phone?  Yes. The whole portal adjusts to phones and tablets; the menu tucks behind the ☰ button.
  • How do I change the language?  Parents can switch between English, Hindi and Bengali from the language box near their name.
  • How do I sign out?  Tap your name card on the dashboard and confirm.
